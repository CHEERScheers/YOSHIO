Molecular Mechatronics Group · NIMS / Hokkaido Univ.

Designing nanostructured liquid crystals
for next-generation soft actuators.

The YOSHIO Lab designs supramolecular liquid crystals and polymer electrolytes that channel ions through self-assembled nanostructures. We translate this molecular control into electromechanical soft actuators, haptic devices, and energy materials at the intersection of chemistry, soft matter physics, and mechatronics.

About the Lab

The YOSHIO Lab is the Molecular Mechatronics Group at the Research Center for Macromolecules and Biomaterials, National Institute for Materials Science (NIMS), led by Dr. Masafumi Yoshio, who concurrently serves as a Professor at the Graduate School of Chemical Sciences and Engineering, Hokkaido University (NIMS Joint Graduate School).

We design supramolecular liquid crystals, ionic polymer electrolytes, and π-conjugated functional materials that exploit nanophase-separated self-assembly to control the transport of ions, electrons, and light. Our work bridges fundamental soft-matter chemistry with next-generation soft actuators, haptic interfaces, sensors, and energy devices.

Research

We pursue interconnected themes that link molecular design, nanostructure formation, and device function — turning supramolecular self-assembly into working materials.

Theme 01

Liquid-Crystalline Ion Conductors

Nanosegregated liquid crystals and ionic liquids that form 1D, 2D, and 3D continuous ion-transport pathways for fast and anisotropic conduction.

Theme 02

Soft Actuators & Haptic Devices

Photopolymerized ionic LC polymer films driven at ≤ 2 V, delivering 100 Hz fast vibration, 5 gf output, and shape-memory function for haptics and soft robotics.

Theme 03

Supramolecular Self-Assembly

Hydrogen-bonded, ion–dipole, and metal-coordination interactions to build columnar, smectic, cubic, and bicontinuous nanostructures with tailored function.

Theme 04

π-Conjugated LC Semiconductors

Ferroelectric and photoconductive π-conjugated liquid crystals — including oxadiazole and thiophene-based polycatenars — for photovoltaics and sensors.

Theme 05

Li-Ion Conducting Phosphate LCs

Phosphate-ester columnar liquid crystals complexed with lithium salts as non-flammable, self-healing electrolyte concepts for next-generation Li batteries.

Theme 06

Mechanochromic Luminescence

Stimuli-responsive emission of hydrogen-bonded benzodithiophene and guanine–oligothiophene assemblies — toward mechanical sensors and optical memory.
